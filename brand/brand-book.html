About this book

Why a brand book.

A brand book is a forcing function. It pins down decisions that would otherwise be re-litigated every time someone makes a slide, sends a press release, or files a grant cover letter.

This book is the public-facing surface of three documents that already exist in the OMNI OS repository: the README.md, docs/01-vision.md, and docs/05-governance.md. It does not invent new positioning. It distills decisions already taken into a form that designers, developers, contributors, and external partners can apply without rereading the entire repository.

It is also a constraint document. The visual system, voice rules, and lexicon defined here are not suggestions. A draft that violates them is a brand error, and brand errors compound — they read as institutional drift, exactly the perception OMNI OS is structurally designed to prevent.

How to use it

  • Developers writing READMEs, OIPs, ADRs, technical docs: refer to §3 (Voice rules) and §6 (Templates). Drop in the README header template and you are 80% there.
  • Designers producing slides, social cards, marketing copy: refer to §4 (Visual system) and §5 (Iconography). Every asset linked is the authoritative source.
  • Foundation staff and trustees producing annual reports, contracts, official correspondence: refer to §1 (Naming architecture) and §6.6 (Email signature). The full lockup with the Dutch legal form is the required first-mention pattern.
  • External partners and journalists: the public version of this book lives at omni-foundation.org/brand (TBD). Press kit is in brand/press-kit/ (Phase 4 deliverable, not yet shipped).

Authority and updates

The authoritative source of every chapter in this book is the linked file in the repository (e.g., brand/STRATEGY.md, brand/colors/tokens.css). Where this book and the source file disagree, the source file is authoritative — but in practice this book is regenerated whenever a source file changes, so the gap should remain zero.

Owner: Lead Architect (cySalazar) for years 1–5. After 2031-05-09, OMNI Foundation Director. Review cadence: annually, or on any brand-affecting decision in /docs/ or /oips/.

Three names. One family.

OMNI operates with three identities serving three distinct purposes — legal, brand, product. This separation lets each be optimized without compromise.

Capitalization rules

  • OMNI is always set in all-caps. Treated as an initialism, not an acronym (we do not expand it).
  • OS is always all-caps when paired with OMNI. The two carry a non-breaking space (OMNI&nbsp;OS).
  • Stichting OMNI — Dutch proper-noun convention; OMNI Foundation — both words initial-cap on the second token.
  • Possessive: OMNI OS's kernel, never OMNI OS' kernel.
"OMNI Foundation legally is Stichting OMNI. The split exists for the same reason Mozilla Foundation is just 'Mozilla' in the world."

01 · Naming & missionStatement
§2 · Mission & vision

One mission. Three voices.

Mission (funders & policy)

OMNI Foundation develops and maintains OMNI OS — an AI-native, privacy-by-construction, decentralized operating system — and stewards its governance in the public interest under an irrevocable Mission Anchor.

Vision (mainstream)

A world where everyone has the full power of modern AI on a computer they actually control.

Purpose narrative (about-page, manifesto)

The current generation of operating systems was designed before modern AI existed. Today AI capabilities are bolted on top — accessed through cloud APIs owned by a handful of companies, with the user's data as the implicit currency. We accept this because the alternative looks like running a model on a laptop with a fraction of the capability.

OMNI OS proposes a different paradigm. AI runs locally by default. The operating system itself is the orchestrator. Computational scale is achieved by federating with other OMNI OS instances over a peer-to-peer mesh — collective compute among people running the same software, not a service rented from a corporation. Privacy is not a setting in a menu; it is the only thing the protocol knows how to do. Non-compliant nodes cannot produce valid messages — it is mathematics, not policy.

We are building for the next 25 years. Stability of design comes before speed of delivery. Forks are welcome. The Foundation is structurally unable to capture the project.

02 · Personality & voiceAttributes
§3 · Brand personality

Five attributes.

Each one tells you how to write a sentence and how to draw a panel.

Patient

25-year horizon phrasing. No urgency. Sunsets and dates anchored. Roadmap by quarter, not by hours.

Rigorous

Every claim names its mechanism. Numbers cited. Specs with line references. Visible grid in design.

Severe

Short sentences. No filler. Uncomfortable truths tolerated. No exclamation marks. Anywhere. Ever.

Open

Document the decision behind the decision. ADRs, OIPs, signed commits. Source files ship next to artefacts.

Honest

Name the risks. AWAITING_CRYPTO_REVIEW markers stay until they don't apply. Status labels are document chrome.

Anti-personality

Never: excited, friendly-startup, cypherpunk-aesthetic, corporate-confident, crypto-finance.

02 · Personality & voiceRules
§4 · The eight voice rules

How OMNI sounds.

  1. Name the mechanism. Don't say "secure"; say what makes it secure.
  2. Short sentences. Then a longer one for rhythm. Average 14–18 words.
  3. No exclamation marks. Ever. Including in error messages, social posts, and informal chat.
  4. No war / religion / sport / family metaphors. No "battle", "tribe", "mission-critical sacred trust".
  5. The reader is competent. Address developers and technical readers as peers.
  6. Sound the same in 5 years. Avoid trend vocabulary ("vibes", "agentic", "moat", "10x").
  7. Hedge precisely or not at all. "Probably secure" is forbidden.
  8. Cite yourself. Use stable file paths, not floating descriptions.

The lexicon — owned vs. rejected

Owned

AI-native · local-first · privacy by construction · mesh · attestation · TEE · peer-to-peer · compliance proof · Mission Anchor · 25-year horizon · OIP · BDFL sunset · forks-welcome · quadratic vote · proof-of-uptime

Rejected

blockchain · token · web3 · DAO · trustless · agentic · vibes · superhuman · 10x · moat · disruptive · bulletproof · military-grade · revolutionary · paradigm shift · mission-critical

Local-first AI for a computer you actually control.

AI as a system primitive — running on hardware you own, federating with peers who prove their compliance.

30-second pitch

OMNI OS is an AI-native operating system. It treats inference, model orchestration, and intelligent agents the way classical operating systems treat processes and files — as kernel-level primitives. AI runs locally by default. When you need more compute than your machine has, OMNI OS federates with other instances over a peer-to-peer mesh of attested nodes — collective compute among people running the same software, not a service you rent. Privacy is enforced cryptographically at the protocol layer. We are building for the next 25 years.

03 · The markConstruction & rules
§6 · Visual identity

Construction & clear-space.

Geometry

  • 6 nodes · 60° spacing · starts top (12 o'clock)
  • Node radius: 8 units (in a 140-unit mark)
  • Core radius: 10 units
  • Ring radius: 52 units
  • Clear-space envelope: 1× mark-height on all sides

Semantics

The core (brick) is the Mission Anchor (Article 3 of the bylaws — irrevocable). The six nodes (petrol) are federated attested peers. The dimming envelope ring signals inclusion without coercion.

What you may never do

Recolor the mark outside the four sanctioned states (full color, monochrome dark, monochrome light, single-ink).

Add effects — drop shadow, glow, bevel, gradient fill, animation in static identity.

Change proportions. Six nodes only. Ring/node/core ratios are fixed.

Flip, rotate, or invert. Orientation is fixed — one node always at 12 o'clock.

Typeset OMNI OS in a different font. Source Serif 4 700 is the only sanctioned wordmark.

Crop the wordmark out of a primary or stacked lockup. Use the monogram if space is tight.

The single-red rule

Brick is the ONLY warm accent in the system. Sage warns/passes, charcoal carries, petrol leads. Brick is reserved for:

  • The Mission Anchor dot in the mark
  • Governance-status pills (Vetoed, Critical)
  • SEC-CRITICAL, AWAITING_CRYPTO_REVIEW markers
  • Roadmap phase indicators that intentionally signal "this matters"

Brick is never decorative. If a designer is "using brick to add warmth", they are misusing it.

Why all three are SIL OFL

The codebase is AGPL-3.0; the protocol specs are CC0. Bundling a proprietary font would create a single non-open dependency in the brand layer that contradicts the project's posture. SIL Open Font License permits redistribution, embedding, modification, and forks. Forks of OMNI OS can ship the same fonts without per-seat licensing.

Co-branding

When acknowledging external entities (Open Philanthropy, NLnet, Mozilla MOSS, etc.), OMNI is always the anchor, the external entity the endorser. Standard format:

OMNI Foundation gratefully acknowledges [Funder] for [grant ID / project name].

Logo lockups: external logo to the right of the OMNI Foundation wordmark, separated by a 2-rem rule. Never composite logos with no separator. Endorsement-of-the-month "trusted by" logo walls: forbidden. Funders are listed in the transparency report, not as decoration.

Forks & derivative works

Per the public trademark policy (Article 10.3 of the bylaws):

  • Protocol-compliant forks may use the OMNI OS name, encouraged to add a distinguishing qualifier (OMNI OS — Acme Edition).
  • Protocol-incompatible forks may not use OMNI OS in their name. They may use derived from OMNI OS.
  • The Foundation will not initiate trademark enforcement against good-faith uses. Bad-faith uses (impersonation, regulatory fraud) addressed case-by-case.

06 · Operating the brandContributing
§12 · Contributing & contact

How to contribute.

Brand changes follow the same review process as code. The brand is documented; the documentation is versioned; changes are signed.

Adding to the brand

  1. If you are adding a new concept to the lexicon, propose it via PR to brand/STRATEGY.md §8 first. Lexicon precedes iconography.
  2. If you are adding a color token, add it to brand/colors/tokens.css + tokens.json + the contrast matrix in palette.md, in that order.
  3. If you are adding a template, follow the consumption pattern documented in brand/templates/README.md.
  4. If you are adding an icon, follow brand/icons/README.md §"Adding a new icon".
  5. Open a Draft PR. Sign your commits (DCO). Brand changes get reviewed by the Lead Architect (for years 1–5) or the Foundation Director (from 2031-05-09 onward).

Reporting brand misuse

Use of the OMNI marks that violates the trademark policy or this book — by good-faith mistake or bad faith — can be reported to brand@omni-foundation.org (email TBD upon Foundation constitution; fall back to cySalazar@cySalazar.com until then). Good-faith corrections are resolved by documentation; bad-faith uses are addressed per the trademark policy.
